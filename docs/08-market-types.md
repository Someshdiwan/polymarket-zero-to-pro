# Market Types on Polymarket

> Not all markets are equal. Each category has distinct characteristics, resolution mechanics, liquidity profiles, and edge opportunities. Understanding where you have an advantage and where you do not is the first filter of professional trading.

---

## Overview

```
Polymarket hosts markets across 8 major categories:

1. Politics
2. Geopolitics & World Events
3. Crypto
4. Finance & Economics
5. Sports
6. Science & Climate
7. Culture & Entertainment
8. Short-Term Price Markets (BTC/ETH Up/Down)

Each category requires a different approach,
different information sources, and different
edge identification strategies.
```

---

## Part 1 : Politics

### What These Markets Cover
```
Elections (national, state, local)
Policy decisions
Legislative outcomes
Leadership changes
Approval ratings milestones
```

### Examples
```
"Who wins the 2026 US Midterms?"
"Will Modi call early elections?"
"Will Trump's tariff bill pass?"
"Will Supreme Court overturn X?"
```

### Edge Sources
```
✅ Deep knowledge of specific political system
✅ Reading primary polling data (not just headlines)
✅ Understanding electoral college / parliamentary math
✅ Following local political journalists
✅ Historical base rates of incumbent performance
```

### Key Characteristics
```
Volume:       Very high ($1M+ on major events)
Time horizon: Days to months
Volatility:   Medium (moves on polls, news)
Resolution:   Clear (official results)
Best for:     Political junkies, policy analysts
```

### Common Mistakes
```
❌ Trusting mainstream media over raw data
❌ Betting based on personal political bias
❌ Ignoring base rates of incumbents winning
❌ Underestimating polling error
```

## Part 2 : Geopolitics & World Events

### What These Markets Cover
```
Military conflicts and strikes
Diplomatic negotiations
International agreements
Leadership survival
Sanctions and treaties
```

### Examples
```
"Will US/Israel strike Iran on March 3?"
"Will Russia-Ukraine ceasefire happen by June?"
"Will North Korea test a nuclear weapon?"
"Will Taiwan strait incident occur in 2026?"
```

### Edge Sources
```
✅ Following specialist geopolitical analysts
✅ Reading military/intelligence publications
✅ Understanding historical conflict escalation patterns
✅ Speed advantage on breaking developments
✅ Knowing resolution criteria precisely
```

### Key Characteristics
```
Volume:       High ($500K+ on active conflicts)
Time horizon: Hours to weeks
Volatility:   Extreme (single event = 50% price swing)
Resolution:   Usually clear, sometimes disputed
Best for:     Geopolitics followers, news readers
```

### Risk Profile
```
⚠️ Highest volatility category
⚠️ Single tweet from a world leader = massive swing
⚠️ Information asymmetry is extreme
⚠️ Market can move 90% in minutes
⚠️ Only trade when you have strong conviction
```

### Real Example from This Repo
```
US/Israel strikes Iran market
March 2 → 97% YES (strike happening)
March 3 → 95% YES
March 5 → 85% YES

Lesson: Market priced correctly
Buying NO at 3¢ = high reward but extremely high risk
Only worthwhile with genuine informational edge
```

### Edge Sources
```

✅ Historical price cycle analysis
```

### Key Characteristics
```
Volume:       High on BTC/ETH, low on alts
Time horizon: Days to months
Volatility:   High
Resolution:   Price = oracle data, events = news
Best for:     Crypto natives, on-chain analysts
```

### Short-Term Price Markets (Special Category)
```
BTC Up or Down — 5 Minutes
BTC Up or Down — 15 Minutes
BTC Up or Down — 1 Hour
ETH Up or Down — 5 Minutes

These are fundamentally different from other crypto markets.
See: 06-candlestick-strategy.md for dedicated guide.

Summary:
→ 5 min = pure noise, never trade
→ 15 min = tradeable with candle strategy
→ 1 hour = most reliable, best edge
```

## Part 4 : Finance & Economics

### What These Markets Cover
```
Central bank decisions (Fed, RBI, ECB)
Inflation and CPI data
GDP and recession calls
Unemployment figures
Stock market milestones
Corporate earnings surprises
```

### Examples
```
"Will Fed cut rates in May 2026?"
"Will US CPI exceed 3% in March?"
"Will S&P 500 reach 7000 by year end?"
"Will US enter recession in 2026?"
"Will RBI cut rates this quarter?"
```

### Edge Sources
```
✅ Understanding macroeconomic data
✅ Following Fed/RBI communications closely
✅ Reading economic research papers
✅ Understanding bond market signals
✅ Historical base rates of central bank behavior
```

### Key Characteristics
```
Volume:       Very high on Fed decisions ($2M+)
Time horizon: Days to months
Volatility:   Medium, spikes on data releases
Resolution:   Extremely clear (official data)
Best for:     Economics students, finance professionals
```

### Why This Category Has Great Edge
```
Market often misprices Fed decisions
Reason: Retail traders rely on media
        Media oversimplifies Fed signals

Edge approach:
→ Read actual Fed statements (not summaries)
→ Understand "dot plot" projections
→ Follow bond market pricing
→ Often diverges from Polymarket consensus
```

```
Volume:       High on major events, low on niche

Volatility:   Medium, spikes on results
```

### India-Specific Opportunity
```
Cricket markets on Polymarket often have:
→ Lower volume than Western sports
→ Less competition from professional traders
→ More mispricing opportunities

If you follow Indian cricket deeply:
→ This is one of your best edge categories
→ Team selection, pitch conditions, weather
→ All factors most Polymarket traders ignore
```

## Part 9 : How to Pick Your Category

### Decision Framework
```
Step 1: What do you consume daily?
        News? Sports? Economics? Crypto?

Step 2: What would you know faster than others?
        Breaking news in your field?

Step 3: What are you willing to research deeply?
        Not just headlines — primary sources

Step 4: Pick 2 categories maximum
        Depth beats breadth in prediction markets

Step 5: Paper trade your categories first
        Track predictions before risking money
```

### Category Recommendations by Profile

| Profile | Primary Category | Secondary Category |
|---|---|---|
| News junkie | Geopolitics | Politics |
| Crypto trader | Crypto (long-term) | Finance |
| Sports analyst | Sports | Culture |
| Economics student | Finance/Economics | Politics |
| Software engineer | Crypto | Tech/Science |
| Indian trader | Cricket | Indian Politics |

## Part 10 : Volume and Liquidity Rules

### Why Volume Matters
```
High volume market ($500K+):
→ Tight bid-ask spread
→ Easy to buy and sell anytime
→ Price reflects genuine consensus
→ Hard to manipulate

Low volume market ($5K):
→ Wide spread = you lose on entry and exit
→ Hard to sell when you want to
→ Single large bet moves price
→ Easier to manipulate
```

### Minimum Volume Rules
```
Short-term trade (hours)  → minimum $100K volume
Medium-term (days)        → minimum $50K volume
Long-term (weeks/months)  → minimum $20K volume
Never trade               → under $5K volume
```
